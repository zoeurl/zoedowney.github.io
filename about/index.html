zoë downey is a photographer and writer based in hertfordshire.

she documents quiet scenes and everyday ephemera that invite digital preservation. she also critiques cinema and contemporary media.

imagery is the concern across her work, paired with the critical discourse that orbits it. her present focus is on minimalist details of still-life and subtler movements of street photography. this process involves zeroing-in on the smaller—but significant—ordinary. she seeks to frame moments of calm, serenity and visual order within the chaos and complexity of modern-day living.

she is a master's graduate of goldsmiths in english and american literature. she specialised in animated films of america and japan.
