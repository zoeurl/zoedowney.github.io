zoë downey (1995) is a photographer and writer based in hertfordshire.

she documents quiet scenes and everyday ephemera that invite digital preservation. her present focus is on subtler movements of street photography and minimalist details of still-life. within the chaos and complexity of modern-day living, she seeks to frame moments of calm, serenity and visual order.

she also critiques cinema and contemporary media. imagery is her concern, paired with the critical discourse that orbits it.

2020: ma american literature and culture—goldsmiths
2017: ba (hons) english—goldsmiths: 1st
